Six classes

Each class is built around one active ability. Movement, weapons, and gunplay are the same for everyone — your ability is your identity.

Phantom

Blink · 12s cooldown

Short-range teleport in the direction you aim. Slips through chokes, escapes bad fights, ends duels you should have lost. Silent footsteps when running.

Rush

Surge · 18s cooldown

2× movement speed for four seconds. Rotate before the enemy team finishes reloading. Faster reload as a passive — Rush wins trades by being where the other team isn't.

Vanguard

Dash ×2 · 8s per charge

Two charges of a directional burst. Peek a corner, dash back. Lateral dodges in 1v1. +15 max HP passive — Vanguard outlasts trades.

Ghost

Cloak · 25s cooldown

Five seconds of invisibility — enemies can't see you, can't engage you. Breaks immediately when you fire. Quieter footsteps as a passive.

Engineer

Barrier · 20s cooldown

Deployable 2.2 × 2m wall. Blocks bullets, blocks movement — yours too, so place it carefully. All ability cooldowns reduced by 15% as a passive.

Hunter

Pulse · 22s cooldown

Radial scan reveals enemy positions through walls for three seconds. Pre-aim before a push, read a flank. Footstep direction indicators are a passive.

Five weapons

Each class can pick any primary. Weapons are the same for everyone — balance shifts with how you play, not what you unlock.

Assault Rifle

9 RPS · Auto · 30 mag

The default. 3-shot body kill, 2-shot with one head. Light vertical recoil. The weapon you reach for when you don't know what's coming.

SMG

14 RPS · Auto · 28 mag

High fire rate, low damage per shot, heavy bloom. Close-range melter — wins corner peeks, useless across the map.

Sniper

1 RPS · Scope · 5 mag

One-shot headshot, two-shot body. FOV-drop scope on right-click. Slow reload. For long lanes and patient players.

Shotgun

1.4 RPS · 9 pellets · 6 mag

Brutal close-range, ineffective past 22m. Reads pellet hits as a single combined damage number so you can feel the spread land.

Pistol

5.5 RPS · Semi · 12 mag

Sidearm — always available. Auto-pulled when your primary mag is empty. Surprisingly viable mid-range with steady aim.

Two maps

Hand-tuned layouts designed for distinct play. Sandstone rewards sightlines and movement skill; Industrial rewards corners and timing.

Sandstone

80×80m · Open desert town

Three sightlines: long lane along the north strip, central plaza with a tower and jump pads, short south alley. Catwalks connect rooftops above the plaza. Snipers thrive here.

Industrial

100×80m · Rusty warehouse

CQB-favored: tight interior corridors, two catwalk levels, shipping containers for cover. One long east-yard sightline. Stairs only — no jump pads. SMG and shotgun shine.

Practice Range

Solo · No bots

A dedicated peaceful area south of the combat zone. Speed test strip, bhop staircase, slide ramps, three jump pads. Tune your movement without dying every six seconds.

How to play

A 60-second crash course.

Movement

WASD to move. Space to jump — hold to bunny-hop and keep your speed across hops. Shift crouches; if you're moving fast it slides. Backward and strafe are slower than forward, like CS. Counter-strafe (tap A then D) snap-stops your sideways momentum for tight peeks.

Combat

LMB to fire, RMB to scope (sniper), R to reload, 1/2/Q to swap weapons. Crouching tightens accuracy; moving widens it. Aim matters.

Abilities

E casts your class ability. Each ability has a cooldown shown on the HUD pill at the bottom of the screen. Use them — they're the difference between a kill and a death.

Online play

Click ⌬ Play Online (FFA) on the main menu. You'll join a free-for-all room with up to 6 players. First to 30 kills wins. No accounts, no matchmaking — just queue and play.

Built for the browser

ILCARTIGO runs in any modern desktop browser with WebGL. No plugins, no downloads, no installer. The whole game ships as roughly 165 KB of gzipped JavaScript — about the same size as a typical news article's worth of images. It loads in under a second on a decent connection.

Movement is CS-style: snappy ground accel, hard friction, counter-strafe, light bunny-hop. Combat is hitscan with per-weapon recoil patterns, spread that grows when you move, and damage falloff with distance. Multiplayer is server-authoritative at 32 Hz with lag-compensated hit detection — same model CS:GO uses.
