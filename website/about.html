About ILCARTIGO

Last updated: 22 May 2026

ILCARTIGO is a free, fast, browser-based arena shooter. It's a personal project — built by one person to be the kind of game I want to play in a browser tab between tasks: short matches, snappy movement, no installer.

The pitch

Most browser shooters either feel mushy or take a minute to load. The goal here is to load in under a second on a decent connection, drop you into a match in two clicks, and feel sharp enough that aim and movement are actually worth practicing. The whole game ships as roughly 165 KB of gzipped JavaScript — small enough to play on bad WiFi.

What's in it

  • Six classes each built around one active ability: Phantom (Blink teleport), Rush (Surge sprint), Vanguard (Dash burst), Ghost (Cloak invisibility), Engineer (Barrier shield wall), Hunter (Pulse wallhack scan).
  • Seven weapons — Assault Rifle, SMG, Sniper, Shotgun, Marksman (semi-auto DMR), LMG, Pistol — each with its own recoil pattern, spread, damage falloff, and feel.
  • Two maps at launch — Sandstone (open desert town, sniper-friendly) and Industrial (rusty warehouse, CQB-favored). A Practice Range mode lets you test movement without dying.
  • Server-authoritative online FFA at 32 Hz with lag-compensated hit detection. Same netcode model CS:GO uses.
  • A real settings menu with FOV, sensitivity, a full crosshair customizer (color, size, thickness, gap, outline, dot toggle), and graphics quality presets.

Under the hood

The client is TypeScript + Three.js + Vite. Movement is a hand-rolled CS-style kinematic controller — counter-strafe, light bunny-hop with a hard speed cap, asymmetric forward/strafe/backward speeds, auto step-up on stairs, slide on Shift while moving fast. No physics library; the math is small and deterministic so it can mirror to the server.

Combat is hitscan. Each weapon defines its recoil pattern, spread, bloom curve, damage falloff, and headshot multiplier in a single config object. The shotgun fires 9 pellets per trigger pull with damage summed into one floating number on hit, like Krunker.

Multiplayer is server-authoritative at 32 Hz over Socket.io. Clients send sequence-numbered inputs and predict locally; the server runs the same movement code and reconciles via snapshots. Hitscan is lag-compensated — the server rewinds targets ~100ms to where they were on the shooter's screen before running the raycast. Class abilities are server-validated: cooldowns, charges, and active state all live on the server.

Design choices

A few things I deliberately said no to:

  • No pay-to-win. Cosmetic skins are the only thing I plan to charge for, if anything. Class abilities and weapons are the same for everyone.
  • No accounts required. Click play, pick a class, you're in a match. If accounts get added later, they'll be optional and only for persisting stats and cosmetics.
  • No mobile. FPS games on mobile browsers are a bad experience. The site is responsive so you can read about the game on your phone, but the game itself is desktop-only.
  • No microtransactions during matches. No loot boxes, no ad-watching for rewards.

How it's funded

The site shows ads — Google AdSense on the homepage and between matches, never overlaying gameplay. Ads pay for the game server. There are no premium subscriptions or pay-to-win mechanics. If ads aren't enough to keep the server running, I'll figure something else out before putting ads in your face mid-fight.
